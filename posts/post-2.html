Performance vs Brand: The False War Killing Indian D2C Companies

May 2025
6 min read
By The Guerilla Marketer

Walk into any D2C founder's Slack channel and you'll eventually hear two camps argue: the performance marketers who want every rupee traceable to a conversion, and the brand builders who talk about equity, narrative, and long-term trust. Both think the other is wasting money. Both are partially right — and the brands that figure this out are quietly building category leadership while everyone else fights over CPMs.

This is not a think-piece about brand versus performance. It is a practical framework for when to use each, in what ratio, and at what stage of your brand's growth.

Why Pure Performance Hits a Wall

If you have been running Meta and Google performance ads for 12 months or more, you have probably noticed something: your CPMs keep rising, your CTRs keep falling, and the audience that used to convert reliably is getting saturated. You refresh creative, you try new formats, you add new interest stacks — and the curve keeps bending the wrong way.

This is not a Meta problem. This is a brand problem wearing a performance costume.

When consumers don't recognise your brand, your ads have to work extremely hard to build context, communicate value, and drive action — all in one creative unit. That's expensive. As you saturate your best audiences, that cost goes up because you're constantly introducing yourself to cold audiences with zero pre-existing trust.

💡 Brands with strong recall convert cold traffic at 2–3x the rate of unknown brands in the same category — with the same ad creative. The difference is entirely brand equity, not media buying skill.

Why Pure Brand Spend Doesn't Pay Rent

On the other side, founders who invest heavily in brand — big content plays, influencer campaigns, brand films, community building — often find themselves with strong awareness metrics and poor revenue numbers. They've built a reputation that isn't converting.

Brand spend without a performance funnel underneath it is like filling a bucket with no bottom. Awareness without intent capture is expensive goodwill.

Performance Only

Short-term wins, CPM inflation over time, audience saturation, no moat. Every competitor can copy your targeting.

Brand Only

Strong recall, poor conversion rates, slow revenue feedback loops, hard to justify to investors and boards.

The Right Ratio at Each Stage

Stage 1: 0 to ₹20L Monthly Revenue

At this stage, survival depends on learning fast. Spend 90% on performance, 10% on brand. You need to find out what messaging converts, which audiences respond, what your real CAC is. Brand investment here is premature — you haven't earned the clarity to invest in it wisely yet.

Stage 2: ₹20L to ₹1Cr Monthly Revenue

This is where most Indian D2C brands plateau. The performance engine is running, but CPMs are rising and growth is slowing. Shift to 75% performance, 25% brand. Start building organic equity: a consistent Instagram presence, a founder story, a content series that earns attention instead of buying it. This brand spend will reduce your CPMs and increase conversion rates on performance campaigns — typically within 60–90 days.

Stage 3: ₹1Cr+ Monthly Revenue

At this scale, brand is a business strategy, not a marketing tactic. Move to 60% performance, 40% brand. Invest in larger content moments, PR, creator ecosystems, and community. Your CAC should be falling even as your spend rises — because brand equity is doing the heavy lifting on cold audiences.

The Practical Takeaway

Don't ask "should we do performance or brand?" Ask: "What ratio do we need at this stage of our growth, and are we measuring each correctly?"

Performance is measured in CAC, ROAS, and contribution margin. Brand is measured in organic traffic trends, direct-to-site visits, branded search volume, and repeat purchase rate. If your brand investment isn't moving any of those numbers over a 90-day window, it's not working — and that's a different conversation.

The brands building lasting businesses in India aren't choosing one or the other. They're sequencing them correctly.
